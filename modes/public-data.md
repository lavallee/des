# Public-data mode

Use for public dashboards, data explorers, scorecards, explainers, maps, and
evidence-led websites where readers may not know the domain vocabulary.

## Default contract

- Variance `medium`; density `balanced`; motion `explanatory`; type register
  `editorial-data`; imagery `evidence-and-explanation`.
- Begin with the question the data can answer, then provide overview,
  comparison, explanation, provenance, and a path to exact values.
- Make population, time basis, units, denominator, update cadence, missingness,
  uncertainty, and methodology visible at the point they affect interpretation.
- Use charts for relationships and tables for lookup. Every visual encoding has
  a text or tabular route to exact values.
- Progressive disclosure may move method detail out of the first viewport, but
  may not hide qualifications that change the claim.
- Filters announce scope and preserve shareable state. Empty results explain
  whether the data is absent or the filter removed it.

## Mode permissions and constraints

- Continuous gradients are allowed when they encode ordered data and have a
  legible scale or legend. Decorative gradients still need a stated compositional
  role and must not masquerade as data.
- Surface elevation may separate map controls, annotations, or overlays when
  position alone is insufficient. Do not turn every statistic into a card.
- Type may be more editorial than an operator tool, while labels, units, and
  numeric comparison remain stable and tabular.
- Explanatory motion may reveal change, sequence, or correspondence. The same
  interpretation must remain available without motion.
- Illustrations and photographs may establish context; data graphics remain
  evidence and must not be decorative wallpaper.

Use the shared gates in `RUBRIC.md`, then the public-data section. Treat source
custody and interpretive honesty as task correctness, not footer polish.
