# Editorial mode

Use for articles, reports, documented records, case studies, investigations,
and long-form narrative where the reader follows a sustained argument.

## Default contract

- Variance `medium`; density `relaxed`; motion `quiet`; type register
  `editorial`; imagery `narrative-and-evidence`.
- Protect a readable measure and a durable hierarchy for headline, deck,
  byline, body, sections, figures, captions, notes, and citations.
- Distinguish observed fact, attributed claim, analysis, and recommendation in
  language and presentation.
- Let figures interrupt the reading column only when their scale or comparison
  benefits. Captions state what the reader is seeing and where it came from.
- Footnotes, bibliographies, corrections, and update timestamps remain
  addressable and linkable.
- Provide navigation for long documents without converting every section into
  an app panel.

## Mode permissions and constraints

- Expressive typography, italics, rules, pull quotes, and shifts in measure are
  allowed when they establish voice or reading rhythm. They are not reserved for
  one house font.
- Cards are for bounded side material, not the default container for prose.
- Motion is quiet by default: progress, anchored navigation, figure transitions,
  or causal explanation. Reading must not depend on scroll-jacking.
- Photography, illustration, and data figures need editorial purpose, source
  treatment, alt text, and captions where necessary.
- Decorative color, texture, gradient, or shadow is allowed when it supports the
  publication's identity without lowering legibility or suggesting false depth.

Use the shared gates in `RUBRIC.md`, then the editorial section. Evaluate the
actual reading sequence at narrow and wide measures, not a stack of component
screenshots.
