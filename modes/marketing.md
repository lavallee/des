# Marketing mode

Use for product sites, launches, campaign pages, and conversion surfaces where a
prospective user must understand distinction, credibility, and next action.

## Default contract

- Variance `high`; density `relaxed`; motion `expressive`; type register
  `branded`; imagery `art-directed`.
- Start from one product argument and one audience tension. Choose a named
  macrostructure before styling; do not default to centered hero, logo strip,
  three feature cards, testimonial wall, and CTA.
- Lead with product-specific language and proof. Never invent customers,
  testimonials, usage counts, benchmarks, logos, or capabilities.
- Demonstrate the product with real interface states, output, process, or
  concrete artifacts when available. Decorative browser chrome is not proof.
- Vary structure across unrelated projects, but preserve design DNA across one
  site's pages.

## Mode permissions and constraints

- Gradients, shadows, expressive type, asymmetry, layered media, illustration,
  and purposeful motion are allowed. Each must support hierarchy, identity,
  product understanding, or the emotional register—not merely signal “designed.”
- A single strong composition is better than a catalog of effects. Do not mix
  multiple unrelated visual languages on one page.
- Motion must preserve content access, reduced-motion behavior, performance, and
  control. Scroll position is not a substitute for product logic.
- Calls to action state their destination or consequence. Urgency and scarcity
  claims require evidence.
- Mobile is a recomposition, not a clipped desktop or a pile of full-width cards.

Use the shared gates in `RUBRIC.md`, then the marketing section. Compare
candidates by argument comprehension, distinctiveness, proof, and action—not by
the number of visual effects or deviation from a template.
